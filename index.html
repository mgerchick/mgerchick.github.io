About Me

I am a data scientist focused on questions at the intersection of machine learning and public policy. My work focuses on algorithmic accountability, explainability, competition, and data privacy. Previously, I was a Technology Fellow in the U.S. Senate, where I worked for the Senate Judiciary Committee Antitrust Subcommittee through the TechCongress fellowship program. My work in the Senate focused on policy related to online safety, algorithmic decision-making, and competition policy. Previously, I was a data scientist at the Stanford Computational Policy Lab. I hold an M.S. in Engineering (Computational Social Science) and a B.S. in Mathematical and Computational Science, both from Stanford University.
